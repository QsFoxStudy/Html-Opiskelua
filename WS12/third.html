Social Behavior

  • Family Groups: Foxes are social animals and live in family groups.
  • Territorial: They are highly territorial, marking their territory with urine and feces.
  • Dominance Hierarchy: Within a group, there is a clear dominance hierarchy, with the dominant pair leading the group.
  • Communication: Foxes communicate through a variety of vocalizations, body language, and scent marking.

Hunting and Diet

  • Omnivores: Foxes are omnivores, meaning they eat both meat and plants.
  • Predators: They are skilled hunters and prey on small mammals, birds, insects, and fruits.
  • Scavengers: They will also scavenge for food, eating carrion and garbage.
